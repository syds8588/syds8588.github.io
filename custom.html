FISH

What fish you choose for your tank creates the feel of your whole aquarium. The size of your aquarium determines what kind of fish you can have and how many.

Skooling fish create a beautiful and simple aesthetic that work well in large groups and open tanks. More fish can be housed in a aquarium as long as they are compatable and aren't aggressive to one another.

The overall your tanks bioload must meet up with the amount of gallons that your aquarium can hold. Some fish have a heavier bioload than others and this will decrease the amount of fish that you can house.

Plants

Beautiful plants provide not only height but beautiful carpeted aquascapes for your tank inhabitants. Different plants need different levels of light and can create beautiful diversity for every tank.

invertibrates

Shrimp and snails are the cleaners of every aquarium. They provide the essential task of helping ot keep a aquarium clean and free of debris.

Shrimp are a beautiful and colorful addition to any aquarium. These little guys are not only fun to watch to also very helpful to the tidiness of the aquarium

  • Cardinal Tetra

    These brightly colored skooling fish add a flash of color and a beautiful grouping. They are happiest in larger groups.

  • Celestial Rasbora

    A beautful yet sublte fish that loves to hide among the plants, happiest in groups these fish love to show off their lovely pattern.

  • Zebra Danio

    Zebra danio are a mid-sized fish and are very active. Their beautiful stripes flash as they dart around their environment.

  • Betta

    Bettas have lots of personality and are definitely a show fish in any aquarium. Male bettas can only be housed one at a time and because of their aggressive nature they are not recommended to be housed with other fish.

  • Moss balls

    These gorgeously elegent moss balls are wildly popular. The come in groups of three and require no additional maintenance.

  • Radican Sword

    Long full leaves add height to the scenery. A great addition that provides a beautiful hidding spot for fish and shrimp.

  • Baby Tears

    These cute little leaves bunch together to create a lush carpet of greenery. A fun addition that can be used to drap around rocks or branches to provide a dense contained area of green thats easy to maintain.

  • Christmas Moss

    Christmas moss provides a lush carpeted look with the added height and texture of the individual fern fronds . A fun addition that can be used to drap around rocks or branches to provide a dense contained area of green thats easy to maintain.

  • Blue Shrimp

    These gorgeous blue shrimp provide a pop of color along with the benefit of cleaning debris from the tank. Best in groups they pair well with skooling fish but shouldn't be housed with bettas.

  • Red Sakura

    These brilliant red shrimp provide a pop of color along with the benefit of cleaning debris from the tank. Best in groups they pair well with skooling fish but shouldn't be housed with bettas.

  • Nerite Snail

    Large round striped shells are a nice addition to any tank. Snails are wonderful for helping to maintain a clean tank.

  • Rabbit Snail

    Beautiful long shell and bright yellow bodies these snails are wonderfully fun to watch move around the tank.
